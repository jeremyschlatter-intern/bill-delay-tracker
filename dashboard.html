      Methodology & Data Notes

      This dashboard tracks the time between when bill text becomes available on Congress.gov and when a CRS (Congressional Research Service) summary is published. CRS summaries are essential for making legislation accessible — they provide plain-language explanations that help staff, journalists, and the public understand what bills actually do.

      Data source: Congress.gov API (api.congress.gov). All bill types from the selected Congress are included.

      Delay calculation: For each bill, we compare the earliest text version publication date against the earliest summary update date. "Pending" bills have published text but no summary yet; their wait time is calculated from text publication to today.

      Page count estimation

      Page counts are estimated from bill text word counts (approximately 275 words per printed page). These are approximations — actual page counts in the printed PDF may differ somewhat. The estimates are used to give a sense of bill complexity and the volume of text awaiting summarization.

      Committee reporting

      A bill is marked as "reported by committee" if its action history includes actions such as "ordered to be reported," "reported favorably," or "committee discharged." These bills have advanced through the committee process and are more likely to receive floor consideration, making the absence of a summary more consequential.
